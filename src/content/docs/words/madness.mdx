### 分析词义

**Madness** 是形容词 **mad** 的名词形式。
1. **医学/精神**: 指严重的精神疾病或失常。
2. **日常隐喻**: 指极其愚蠢、不理智或危险的行为（如 It would be sheer madness to go out in this storm）。
3. **情绪**: 极度的愤怒。

### 列举例句

1. "There's a method in his **madness**." (他的疯狂中自有其道理/乱中有序。)
2. "It's pure **madness** to spend all your savings on a single lottery ticket." (把所有积蓄都花在一张彩票上纯粹是疯了。)
3. "The crowd broke into a state of **madness** when the goal was scored." (进球时，人群陷入了狂热状态。)

### 词根分析

- **mad**: 源自古英语 *gemæd(e)d*，意为“发疯的”。
- **-ness**: 名词后缀。

### 词缀分析

- **后缀**: **-ness**，表示性质或状态。

### 文化背景

短语 "Method in one's madness" 出自莎士比亚的《哈姆雷特》，意指某人的行为看似疯狂，其实背后有其合理的逻辑。

### 单词变形

- 相关形容词: **mad**
- 副词形式: **madly**

### 记忆辅助

**Mad**（疯的）+ **-ness**（名词后缀）= 疯狂。

### 助记故事

The scientist worked in his dark lab for three days without sleeping. People said it was **madness**. They thought he was losing his mind. But when he finally stepped out with a cure for the disease, they realized his **madness** was actually genius. Sometimes, the line between **madness** and greatness is very thin.
